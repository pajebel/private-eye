About Us

Welcome to EYE INVESTIGATION AGENCY, the trusted source for all your investigative needs. Our team of experienced and skilled private investigators are dedicated to providing you with professional and confidential services. Whether you require surveillance, background checks, or asset searches, we have the expertise to deliver accurate and reliable results. Rest assured that your privacy and satisfaction are our top priorities. Contact us today to discuss your case and let us uncover the truth for you.

EYE INVESTIGATION AGENCY is a Gambian owned company. It is the first of its kind in the country. Our goal is to provide excellent investigative service to Individuals, Public and Private Entities such as Local Government Offices, Human Rights Organizations, and Non-Governmental Organizations. We provide services in the following areas; BANK FRAUD, INSURANCE FRAUD, ALL WHITE COLOR CRIMES, INFIDELITY/CHEATING SPOUSES, LOSS OF MONEY THROUGH SCAM, CRIMINAL and CIVIL MATTERS, HIDDEN ASSETS, INVENTORY FRAUD, FINANCIAL FRAUD, DUE DILIGENCE, DOMESTIC VIOLENCE, SEXUAL ASSUALT, SEXUAL HARASSMENT, PARENTAL NEGLECT, DISCRIMINATION, FORCED and EARLY CHILD MARRIAGES, LOST ORIGINAL DUCUMENTS such as MARRIAGE and DIVORCE CERTIFICATES, LEASE DOCUMENTS, PROPERTY TITLE DOCUMENTS, BIRTH and DEATH CERTIFICATES and much more . Our INVESTIGATIVE CONSULTANTS are all highly experienced investigators and therefore, should you need our services, please contact us at + 220 514-3395 and + 220 723-9014. Email: peye9709@yahoo.com. We look forward to serving you.

EYE INVESTIGATION AGENCY is currently located at Kanifing East Estate, Serrekunda. It is a Limited Liability Company founded by Mr. KEBBA CEESAY. The Shareholders of the company are KEBBA CEESAY, MOMODOU CANTEH and OMAR GASSAMA with OUSMAN CEESAY as Company Secretary. The Agency was registered and issued the 'CERTIFICATE of BUSINESS REGISTRATION' on October 13, 2022. The Founder and Majority shareholder, Mr. Kebba Ceesay, holds an Applied Business Associate Degree in Private Investigation from the ACADEMY OF COURT REPORTING (A Legal Institute) located in Cincinnati, Ohio in the United States of America. Prior to Mr. Ceesay's migration to the United States of America in August of 1993, he worked at the International Bank for Commerce and Industry (B.I.C.I.) from 1975 to 1979, where he rose in rank from Bank Clerk to Branch Manager of the Bank's Branch in Bakau. From 1979 to 1984. Mr. Ceesay worked at Youth Front Against Drugs and Alcohol, a Non-Governmental Organization sponsored by the Swedish International Development and Cooperation Agency (SIDA) as Administrative Secretary. During his tenure at Youth Front Against Drugs and Alcohol, Mr. Ceesay organized symposiums where he invited doctors, such as late Dr. Jack Faal of Ndenban Hospital, late Dr.Oldfield (Royal Victoria Hospital which is now Edward Francis Small Teaching Hospital) and Dr. Tumani Cora (Scientist) of the Medical Research Council, Gambia (MRC) and Sociologist/Politician Hon. Halifa Sallah, to talk to youths about the dangers of Alcohol and Drug Abuse. Between 1984 and 1993, Mr. Ceesay worked at the Office of the Auditor General of The Gambia (now known as National Audit Office). At the office of the Auditor General of The Gambia, he started as Audit Trainee but rose in rank to the Senior Position of Auditor. From 1984 to 1989 during his tenure at the Office of the Auditor General, Mr. Ceesay attended several Auditing Seminars at the Management Development Institute (now a branch of the University of The Gambia) on topics such as Revenue Audit, Vouching and Budgeting. However, in 1992 Mr. Ceesay was fortunate to receive an overseas training in Cairo, Egypt on Budgeting and Financial Management, sponsored by Sahel Regional Financial Management Project from Canada. Prior to the Cairo training, Mr. Ceesay attended a six-week training course on the topic, Audit of Public Enterprises at the Indian Institute of Public Administration in New Delhi, India under the sponsorship of The Comptroller and Auditor General of India. Whilst working at the Auditor General's Office, between 1987 and 1993, Mr. Ceesay worked in the Program Development Unit which was responsible for the audit of programs and projects funded by international organizations such as World Bank, United Nations, USAID and IMF
